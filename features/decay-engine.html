For Data Teams

Five layers. Every one earns its place.

Here's the implementation. Not hiding it — we want you to know the engineering is real. We weight five signals. The third one is the one nobody else has.

01

EWMA Smoothing

40% weight

Two exponentially-weighted moving averages on weekly clicks: short window α=0.4 (~4-week half-life) and long window α=0.15 (~12-week half-life). Crossover signal fires when EWMA_short < EWMA_long × 0.85. We chose EWMA over SMA because a single anomalous week (server outage, holiday) doesn't trigger noise. The candidate set is gated here before any expensive computation runs downstream.

02

OLS Regression Velocity

Significance gate

Linear regression clicks ~ week_index over the last 16 weeks. We extract slope β and p-value with Benjamini-Hochberg correction across the full tracked set. Real decay requires β < 0 and p < 0.05 — the trend must be statistically significant, not just directional. A −10% drop could be one bad week or a real trend. OLS with significance testing separates signal from noise.

03

Category Performance Index (CPI)

The one nobody else has

We aggregate weekly trends across all pages in a category (e.g., "AI tools," "CRM software") and compute relative_velocity = page_slope − category_slope. If your "ChatGPT alternatives" page is down 20% but the category is down 35%, you're winning share — no alert. If your page is down 10% while the category is up 5%, you're losing share fast — alert. This is the difference between "decay" and "demand death." Every competitor who fires on a category death event is destroying customer trust. We don't.

04

STL Seasonal Decomposition

20% weight

We decompose each time series into Trend + Seasonal + Residual using a 52-week seasonal period. The current residual is compared to the historical residual band — only deviations outside the band contribute to the decay score. Your Halloween page tanking in November is expected seasonal behavior, not decay. Alerting on it would be a false positive that trains users to ignore alerts. We strip it.

05

Bayesian Beta-Binomial Credible Intervals

10% weight

CTR is modeled as a Beta distribution with a prior derived from category averages (Beta(α, β) informed by peer pages). Position uncertainty is Gaussian. We combine to produce a credible interval on the decay score. "Score 87 ± 4 (90% CI)" is actionable. "Score 87 ± 22 (90% CI)" means insufficient data — the UI shows you the range, not a fake point estimate. We use a conjugate model because it updates correctly with new data and has closed-form posteriors — no MCMC overhead at scale.

score = 0.40 × velocity_signal + 0.30 × relative_cpi + 0.20 × seasonal_residual + 0.10 × confidence_factor

Honest Limits

What Decay Engine doesn't do

Pages with fewer than 12 weeks of GSC data don't get a decay score — the model defaults to the category baseline and shows you the uncertainty band. We don't manufacture confidence we don't have. If your site has high week-to-week volatility from very low traffic, scores will have wide intervals — and we show you the width, not just the midpoint. Decay Engine also doesn't diagnose why a page is decaying (that's LLM Citation Gap + AI Overview Infiltrator's job) — it tells you a page is decaying with statistical confidence.
