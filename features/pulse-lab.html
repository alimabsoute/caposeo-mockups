For Data Teams

Four signals. One number. All the uncertainty shown.

Search Demand Signal

Google Trends relative interest, z-scored against 90-day baseline for the same keyword. We account for the fact that Trends indices are relative, not absolute — normalizing against baseline removes that ambiguity.

Social Signal

Reddit submission velocity (new posts/hour) plus comment delta (engagement acceleration). Both z-scored. We use Reddit because it's the leading indicator for organic search spikes by 2–4 days on average.

Editorial Signal

News mention acceleration via Brave News API — not count, but velocity. A sudden increase in news coverage about a topic is a leading indicator that search demand is about to follow.

SERP Volatility Signal

Results churn on the target SERP. High churn = Google isn't satisfied with current results = opportunity window is open. Measured as Spearman rank correlation between consecutive daily SERPs.

pulse_score = Σ (w_i × zscore_i) for i ∈ {search, social, editorial, serp} · each w_i learned from historical predictive accuracy

Honest Limits

What Pulse Lab doesn't do

Pulse scores are leading indicators, not guarantees. We surface signals that historically precede traffic spikes — but a topic can surge on social and fail to convert to search demand. The score quantifies signal strength, not certainty. For low-volume niche topics (<500 monthly searches), the SERP volatility signal is unreliable and is down-weighted automatically. We also don't predict the magnitude of a trend — only its direction and velocity. "Rising fast" ≠ "will become your highest-traffic piece."
